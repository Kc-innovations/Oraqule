Cross River National Park, located in southeastern Nigeria's Cross River State, is renowned for its rich biodiversity and expansive tropical rainforests. Covering approximately 4,000 square kilometers, it stands as Nigeria's largest area of closed-canopy rainforest and is recognized as a global biodiversity hotspot .


Park structure and divisions


The park comprises two distinct, non-contiguous divisions:

  • Oban Division: Situated in the southern part of the park, this division encompasses about 3,000 km² of lowland rainforest. It shares a border with Cameroon's Korup National Park, forming a transboundary conservation area. The terrain is characterized by hills rising up to 1,000 meters, serving as a crucial watershed for rivers such as the Calabar, Kwa, and Korup
  • Okwangwo Division: Located in the northern sector, this division spans approximately 640 km². It features rugged terrain with elevations ranging from 150 meters in the lowlands to about 1,700 meters near the Obudu Plateau. This area is part of a larger biosphere corridor that includes the Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary and Cameroon's Takamanda National Park

Biodiversity and Conservation.

    Cross River National Park is home to an impressive array of wildlife, including:

  • Primates: The park supports 16 recorded primate species, such as the endangered Cross River gorilla (Gorilla gorilla diehli), chimpanzees, drills, and Preuss’s red colobus monkey .
  • Other Fauna: The park harbors forest elephants, various antelope species, and over 950 butterfly species, highlighting its ecological richness
  • Flora: The diverse vegetation includes over 1,500 species of vascular plants, with several endemic to the region. Notably, species like Prunus africana are valued for their medicinal properties .
